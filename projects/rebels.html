ReBELS

Resolving Biological carbon Export in the Labrador Sea

Duration: 2023-2028
Funding: NERC Standard Grant, £1.05M
Role: Co-PI, WP1-2 lead
Project Website →

Project Overview

The Labrador Sea is one of the few regions in the global ocean where deep winter convection brings nutrient-rich waters to the surface, fueling intense spring phytoplankton blooms. These blooms drive significant biological carbon export, yet the mechanisms controlling export efficiency—and how they might change with climate—remain poorly understood.

ReBELS combines autonomous platforms (ocean gliders, biogeochemical floats) with ship-based observations to capture the full seasonal cycle from winter mixing through spring bloom development and summer stratification. By observing continuously over multiple years, we aim to resolve how physical forcing regulates particle formation, export flux, and remineralization depth in this critical region for ocean carbon storage.

Key questions include: How does convective mixing depth set biological community structure and export? What role do mesoscale eddies play in modulating productivity and particle sinking? How efficiently is carbon transferred from the surface to depth, and what controls variability between years?
